As part of the Personal Finance Administration service (PFA), STL is responsible for paying any Medical expenses on behalf of the client.  An extension to this responsibility involves ensuring that a schedule payment gap is claimed from Medicare and the client reimbursed.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The manual process of managing and tracking outstanding claims was time consuming and labour intensive.  Over time, the tracking mechanisms implemented (in Excel) has not scaled, and has buckled with the quantity of claims.  This has resulted outstanding claims in the order of millions of dollars.  Whilst the financial benefit to STL is relatively negligible, STL has a clear duty of care to ensure that their clients receive all the funds that they are entitled to.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>This process was chosen from a list of candidate processes as it is relatively simple, and provided an opportunity to demonstrate the capabilities of the oracle BPM tool.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>At a high level, the process is triggered from the payment of an invoice from a medical practice (or from a manual screen to trigger ad-hoc back log claims), and tracks the request for receipt of payment from the medical practice, through to the submission of the claim to Medicare.  The process automatically populates the Medicare form with client details, further alleviating manual workload.<br /></p>
