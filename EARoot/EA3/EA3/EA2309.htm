Metadata is defined as data providing information about one or more aspects of the data, such as:<br /></p><p><ul><li>Means of creation of the data</li><br /></p><p><li>Purpose of the data</li><br /></p><p><li>Time and date of creation</li><br /></p><p><li>Creator or author of data</li><br /></p><p><li>Location of data</li><br /></p><p><li>Standards used</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p>It defines how the development methodology produces meta-data and the standards that should be applied to that meta-data at each stage.<br /></p><p>An essential component of a data warehouse/business intelligence system is the metadata and tools to manage and retrieve metadata.  Meta data has been described as the DNA of a data warehouse, as it defines the elements of the data warehouse and how they work together.  <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Meta data provides descriptors to turn data into information<br /></p>
