This layer represents "as-is” services (or application business logic) located outside of the STL SOA Platform.  From a SOA Reference Architecture perspective, this is the lowest level of granularity we can go down to. Services in this layer are typically provided by existing applications, these include: <br /></p><p><ul><li>Packaged Applications such as Oracle E-Business Suite, Salesforce or Objective. </li><br /></p><p><li>Custom applications developed in-house using technologies such as Java, .Net, Oracle Forms, PL/SQL</li><br /></p><p><li>External Services provided by other 3<sup>rd</sup> parties, e.g. services provided by partners, suppliers, customers, etc. as well as software as a service (SaaS).</li><br /></p><p></ul>There is a considerable amount of variety in this layer, including, but not limited to:<br /></p><p><ul><li>Database access</li><br /></p><p><li>File Exchange</li><br /></p><p><li>Messaging (JMS, MQ, AQ)</li><br /></p><p><li>Web or REST Services</li><br /></p><p><li>OPC (Open Process Control)</li><br /></p><p></ul>Services located within the Application Services layer MUST ONLY be invoked by a service located in the Internal Virtual Services layer.<br /></p>
