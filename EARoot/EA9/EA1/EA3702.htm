This pattern differs from the previous two patterns in that the number of copies of an activity cannot be determined before the copies are to be created.  The exact number is actually determined during the performance of those copies.  This means that a standard looping or multi-instance pattern will not be sufficient to create this behaviour. A more complex form of looping is needed.  A specific pattern of activities and flow control mechanisms are required to create this pattern.  The target activity (Activity 2) must be paired with another activity (Activity 3) that will determine if another copy of the target activity is required.  These two activities are placed within a loop.  The completion of the target activity is not required before the loop is iterated.  However, all the copies of the target activity (Activity 2) must be completed before the final activity (activity 4) can be started.
