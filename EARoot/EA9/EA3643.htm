This is a catch-all term used to describe a service that doesn’t fit in one of the other service layers (i.e. Internal Virtual Service, Business Process, and External Virtual Service).<br /></p><p>As such this layer is further divided into the following functional categories; Entity Service, Functional Service, Task Based Service and Utility Service.<br /></p><p>Each business service must provide a concrete implementation of an abstract WSDL that has been designed in accordance with the STL WSDL Design Standards.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><ol><li><ol><li><ol><li><ol><li><font color="#943634"><b>Entity Services</b></font></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol><ol><li><ol><li><ol><li><ol><li><br /></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol>Also known as data services, these services emulate business objects within the enterprise; for example Customer, Product etc.<br /></p><p>The most common use of Entity services is to represent data entities held in a relational database and provides the corresponding lifecycle (i.e. create, read, update and delete) and query operations.<br /></p><p>Entity services can be used within multiple contexts; this makes them excellent candidates for re-use.  <br /></p><p>To promote reuse, the amount of business specific logic (such as validation) within the service should be minimized.  For this reason, an Entity Service should not be directly exposed to the external service layer; rather you would use it within a task based service or business process.<br /></p><p>Entity services tend to be named after the entities they operate on, and must not provide any ‘logic’, just operations on the entity, such as the standard CRUD operations (and their variances). Any logic must be provided in a Task Service, as detailed below.<br /></p><p><font color="#943634"><b><br /></p><p></b></font><font color="#943634"><b>Functional Services</b></font><br /></p><p><ol><li><ol><li><br /></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol>It is common to embed complex rules, algorithms, etc. directly within a task based (or even entity) service. This can result in duplicate/similar functionality being re-implemented in other services and not always consistently.<br /></p><p>This logic should be split out as a standalone functional service.  This will ensure it’s used in a consistent fashion, and also make it simpler to modify (i.e. we only have to do it once), thus increasing the agility of the solution.<br /></p><p>Functional services should NOT use entity services (or access an external data source directly). If you have a functional service that does this, then you have a task based service with functional logic embedded within it. <br /></p><p>In some cases this is allowable; but should be first approved by the STL ICC as this could potentially result in duplicate/similar functionality being re-implemented in other services.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><font color="#943634"><b>Task Based Services</b></font><br /></p><p><ol><li><ol><li><br /></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol>Task Based services are designed to carry out a specific set of related activities, often within the context of a business process.  By their nature they will often act across one or more entity services, and may also make use of other task based or functional services.<br /></p><p>As a result Task Bases Services are often poor candidates for re-use.<br /></p><p><font color="#943634"><b><br /></p><p></b></font><font color="#943634"><b>Common Services</b></font><br /></p><p><ol><li><ol><li><br /></li><br /></p><p></ol></li><br /></p><p></ol>Common Services are application agnostic.  For example user notification, audit, logging; these are likely to be re-used by multiple applications within our infrastructure.<br /></p><p>Whilst these are task based in nature, they tend to provide technical services, which are strong candidates for re-use. When such a service is required, the STL ICC should be consulted for details on whether such a service already exists (or is under development).<br /></p><p>If a required service does not exist, then the STL ICC should be consulted for guidance on how to implement the service.<br /></p>
